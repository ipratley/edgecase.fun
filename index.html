The story

In 2025, Ian Pratley — former RAF officer, 25-year enterprise technology veteran — decided to build an algorithmic trading company from scratch using AI agents to do most of the work. The company is called IPQI. The plan was elegant: prove edge on personal capital, build a track record, attract external funding, scale.

"The model was validated. The checklist had been checked. Thirty instruments were deployed live. All thirty failed immediately."

What followed was a daily collision between human confidence and machine indifference. Between checklists that had been checked and instruments that did not exist. Between governance frameworks built at 2am and prop firm rules discovered at 2:01am.

The Edge Case documents what actually happens when you try to build a serious systematic trading operation with four AI agents, a VPS in London, and a very large amount of optimism. Every episode is based on a real event. The disasters are real. The checklist was real. The instruments were not.
